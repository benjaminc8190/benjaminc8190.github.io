Freedom Project

Context/Content:

The Freedom Project was a year long project which started with picking a topic to research, then learn about our html and css, and choose a tool to learn. For the Freedom Project, my partner (Shelly) and I worked on a website about a topic we chose, Gambling.The tool I choose was materialize, a framework, to help organize my website making it more responsive. Some components of the tool that I used include the navbar, cards, and parallax.

Reflection

I faced many challenges when creating my website, including coloring and decent images. For my mvp(most viable product), I had a white background and the images for my parallax were really blurry. I didn't see it that way at first, but though peer feedback, I was able to notice such mistakes. To fix this, my partner changed the colors to gradience while I worked on finding new images. Thus my takeaway, without the help of feedback from my peers, I would've been to focused on the content to notice that others didn't like my blurry images and my background would've been bland. THis help me realize the importance of peers as they can see things from an audience perspective that I would've missed from focusing on something else.
