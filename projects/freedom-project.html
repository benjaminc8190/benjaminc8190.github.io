Freedom Project

Context: The “Freedom Project” for SEP10 (Software engineering program class tenth grade) is about making an informative website on benefits and technology.


Content: The Freedom Project was a year long project which started with picking a topic to research, then learn about our html and css, and choose a tool to learn. For the Freedom Project, my partner (Shelly) and I worked on a website about a topic we chose, Gambling.The tool I choose was materialize, a framework, to help organize my website making it more responsive. Some components of the tool that I used include the navbar, cards, and parallax.


Challenge: A challenge I had was trying to get the correct padding for my cards to look good in PC and mobile screen sizes.


Takeaway: Through this project, I learned that peer feedback can give you perspectives that I may have missed. For example, my mvp(most viable product) had a white background and the images for my parallax were really blurry. However, I was too focused on fixing the cards to notice this erorr until my classmates pointed it out.


Next steps: With the realization of the importance of peer feedback, I will value it more in future projects.
